## Summary

This case involved a terrorist bomb cell operating across the Southern Border Provinces of Thailand (ยะลา, ปัตตานี, นราธิวาส). The cell was planning a coordinated improvised explosive device (IED) attack on Yala Central Market during peak hours.

### Key Plot Points

- **Target:** Yala Central Market (ตลาดสดเทศบาลเมืองยะลา)
- **Planned Attack Date:** Day 42 of investigation
- **Intercepted:** Day 38 following intelligence from multiple sources

The cell consisted of four members with distinct roles: a bomb maker (primary suspect), a financier, a scout/coordinator, and a courier/driver. Investigation revealed sophisticated operational security including coded communications, distributed procurement of precursor chemicals, and reconnaissance of the target location.

### Outcome

- **Day 38:** DSI tactical unit conducted coordinated raids at four locations simultaneously
- **Bomb Status:** IED successfully defused at target location (vehicle-borne device, 25kg explosive charge)
- **Arrests:** All 4 suspects apprehended without casualties
- **Evidence:** 47 items seized including precursor chemicals, electronic components, vehicle, and communication devices
